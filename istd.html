Succession in Irish Farming

Print, Typography, Photography

  1. This project is a response to the 2015 International Society of Typographic Designers brief ‘Taboo’.
    The project recieved an ISTD award.
  2. Breaking a taboo is usually considered objectionable by society in general.
    Taboos are preset in virtually all societies.

    This project discusses the taboo of succession in Irish family farms, the challenges faced by farm successors in Ireland today, and the importance of
    farming to Irish people and Ireland. This piece, which is aimed at an Irish audience, highlights some of the concerns of successors who will inherit the
    family farm. The topic of inheritance and of succession in farming families is generally a taboo subject, and therefore rarely discussed within the family.
